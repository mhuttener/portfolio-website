Out N About | OnA

Responsive web app for event location-based

UX/UI Design | Research | Branding | Wireframing | Prototyping

Background

I started this project inspired to create a seamless and enjoyable experience for users when discovering and acquiring event tickets.

I recognized that the current process can often be frustrating and wanted to address the underlying issues that were causing this.

After conducting thorough research, I identified several key problems that were contributing to the poor user experience, such as limited payment options, inadequate customer support, and cumbersome login/sign-in procedures.

About | OnA

Out and About - OnA - is a responsive web application that helps users discover events and activities nearby and simplifies ticket purchases. The app provides real-time information about events and activities based on the user's location.

Objective

Provide a seamless booking and payment process and offer personalized events recommendations.

Help users discover nearby events and activities that match their interests and preferences.

Research

Goals

Below are the areas I’d like to explore during the user research. I want to understand:

  1. The patterns behind the event ticket purchasing process.
  2. What are the user’s motivations behind attending specific events and purchasing tickets?
  3. The context in which the users seek information on events and ticket details.
  4. The user’s goals when using an event-location-based app for ticket acquisition, such as finding events with available tickets, comparing prices, and accessing tickets after purchase.

Methodologies

Competitor research

I researched competitors such as Dice and TripAdvisor, and utilized user psychology techniques. I also conducted a SWOT analysis and evaluated UX for the project.

User research

I conducted user interviews to investigate the pain points, goals, response patterns, and noteworthy quotes from users when using an online platform to discover a new event.

User personas

To better understand user groups, I created fictional representations that captured the essence of their demographics, end goals, pain points, and quotes. These representations included scenarios that provided insight into the users' experiences.

Hypothesis

People who often attend events can use an event-based location app to find, organize, and get to events. This app helps them save time and effort by providing all the necessary information in one place.

Research Synopsis

I was surprised to learn that most of the users (even experienced event-goers) tend to make spontaneous decisions when it comes to attending events and purchasing tickets.

They are often influenced by external factors, such as social media ads, last-minute plans with friends, or stumbling upon an event while browsing event-location-based apps.

This led me to discover that what would help the users to set themselves up for long-term success, would be to provide them with better information and tools to make informed decisions before purchasing tickets, such as event details, location, price, and reviews.

MVP Documentation

OnA user profile

People who seek events/concerts to fulfill their entertainment needs. Those on a budget search for deals/discounts for the best value. Those with more money look for VIP/exclusive experiences for a better event-going experience.

MVP Objectives for OnA

  1. Help users discover nearby events and activities that match their interests and preferences.
  2. Provide a seamless booking and payment process and offer personalized events recommendations.
  3. Enable users to share and collaborate with friends and family events or activities nearby.
  4. Facilitates communication and coordination between event organizers and attendees and provides real-time updates and alerts for changes or updates related to scheduled events.

Design System

I developed a Design System that reflects the brand adjectives of OnA. My approach prioritizes a user-centered design, taking into consideration the needs and preferences of OnA's target audience: the event-goers.

The OnA brand identity draws inspiration from music, festivals, and social events, which bring people together and create a sense of joy and happiness.

By carefully considering the brand's inspiration and translating it into visual design elements, I have developed a design that captures the essence of OnA and creates a memorable user experience.

The logo for the Out and About web app, which focuses on events, is designed to capture the excitement and energy of live music concerts and other events.

The logo's icon represents the expression of joy and symbolizes the dynamic energy and excitement of attending a live music event.

Usability Testing

I tested the first version of the prototype with 5 users to determine the necessary revisions to make the overall experience more intuitive and pleasant. Here are some outtakes.

Main pain points that were discovered during the tests and addressed in the iteration:

  • Users had difficulties interpreting some of the prompts on the onboarding page. Fix: Simplified the registration process by reducing the number of required fields.
  • Users encountered instances where event information, such as dates, times, or locations, was inconsistent or missing, leading to confusion. Fix: Improved data consistency and accuracy.
  • Some users found the ticket selection process confusing or cumbersome, especially when events offered multiple ticket types. Fix: Provided clear and concise ticket descriptions.

Final Considerations

Throughout the project, I was responsible for both the UX and UI aspects. I conducted extensive research and rigorous testing to gather valuable insights and feedback.

To decide on the MVP features and functionality, I started by understanding the project's goals and priorities. I identified the core objectives and needs of the users, and then assessed which features and functionality were essential to achieving those objectives. I prioritized those features based on their importance to the project's success and the users' needs.

Additionally, I solicited feedback from users to ensure that I was on the right track and made any necessary adjustments. By following this process, I was able to develop an MVP that was both functional and met the needs of the project and its users.

Finally, I curated my findings and work, ensuring that everything was clearly documented and presented in a visually appealing and easy-to-understand format.
